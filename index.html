✦ Issue #002 — 11 June 2026

Write better essays with AI (for real)

The prompt that does half your essay work

🧠 This Week's Focus

Most students use AI wrong — they paste in the question and hope for a full essay back. That gets you generic output that sounds nothing like you.

The right way is to use AI as a thinking partner. Paste your essay question, ask it for 5 possible arguments, the strongest counterargument to each, and which angle is most original. A proper plan in under 2 minutes.

"Give me 5 distinct arguments I could make for [question], the best counterargument to each, and which angle is most original or underused."

⚡ Quick Hits

Grammarly + AI
The free tier now flags unclear arguments, not just grammar. Run your draft through it before submission. Takes 3 minutes.

Perplexity for sources
Ask: "what are the main academic debates around [topic]?" It pulls real sources with links. Always verify before citing.

The 25-minute rule
Set a timer. Write for 25 minutes without editing. AI helps you plan — the writing still has to come from you.
